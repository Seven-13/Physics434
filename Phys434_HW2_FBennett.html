Problem 1
For each die, there is a one-in-six chance that the die will land on "1." We multiply each chance to get the likelihood that both will roll a 1:
To consider the probability of rolling a 7, we must account for each sum that can give a 7: 1 and 6, 2 and 5, and 3 and 4. Therefore, there are six combinations that give a "7" for the two die,
there is a one-in-six chance of getting a 7 rolled.
It is six times more likely that a sum of seven will be rolled than for two one's, or a sum of two, to be rolled. Hence the ratio is 1:6

Problem 5
Because a Gaussian is a symmetric distribution, taking it's average or sum will still give a Gaussian. The general pattern of the distribution will not change. The standard deviation will not change, because the standard deviation is a measurement of the data that falls within a distance of the mean.
